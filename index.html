About Me

I am a hydrologist with expertise in flood modeling, risk assessment, and operational forecasting systems. With a PhD in Civil and Environmental Engineering from Northeastern University, I specialize in combining traditional hydrologic methods with advanced AI techniques to improve flood prediction and disaster preparedness.

My work spans flood depth estimation using remote sensing, real-time flood forecasting for river systems, and precipitation nowcasting. I have implemented operational systems with Tennessee Valley Authority for flood management and hydropower operations, and analyzed major flood events globally.

Currently serving as a Postdoctoral Research Fellow at the Institute for Experiential AI, I work at the intersection of hydrology, remote sensing, and data science to build tools that help communities prepare for and respond to flood hazards. My research has been published in Nature journals and presented to stakeholders including federal agencies, water utilities, and emergency management organizations.
